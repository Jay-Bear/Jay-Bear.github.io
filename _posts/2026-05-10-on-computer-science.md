# On Computer Science

Years ago, I was an impressionable teenager deciding on a degree to study and
a university to study that degree at. I was stuck deciding between mathematics
and computer science, and unfortunately I chose the latter.

At the time I was reasonably confident in my choice. While I did consider
mathematics, I believed I made the right choice going with computer science...
until half-way through the first semester. I realized it was far *far* less
theoretical than I had been lead to believe. The student helper at the open day
in my memories telling me *"oh yeah there's loads of theory"*; in retrospect
they were probably doing the degree just for programming and hated **any**
level of theory. There were... a lot of those kinds of students during the
degree.

Anyway, my personal tutor advised me against switching degree. "You can pick
more theory in third and fourth year". By *more* he meant like... one or two
classes, but I didn't realize that at the time. I would also come to find that
these supposed 'mathematical' or 'theoretical' modules were often *quite
neither*. It appears that, to a computer scientist, something being
'mathematical' means there's just some equations and maybe a derivation or two.
Mathematics is just the tool to do something.

Picking computer science as a degree is the greatest regret of my life. I
***should*** have picked mathematics. Why didn't I?

School. Specifically the education system, at least here. Mathematics education
before university focuses almost entirely on numbers and using them. If not
numbers, basic algebraic concepts or geometry, but the core issue is that it
focuses on **using them**. Not deriving them, not discovering them, not proving
properties of them, but just... just... ***using them***. They say there's
'problem solving' but where? Plugging in numbers to some method that's been
forced into kids' craniums by rote memorization is not 'problem solving'.
Mathematics is a subject cursed to be shit because of the over-emphasis of
numerical skills. Yeah, sure, they're useful for most of the populace but
*damn* you don't have to kill an entire subject for that.

But guess what *does* allow problem solving... computer science. Kids aren't
taught to memorize specific programs (most of the time), but must instead
figure out their *own* programs to solve given tasks. Any child just itching to
solve problems will find it here instead of math. It makes me wonder just how
many great mathematical minds we're losing to computer science, and the worst
part is they don't even know it. They likely think mathematics is just
arithmetic with letters sometimes. They likely think it's just a tool to do
something. They *think it's just a tool to do something*.

I hate writing code. I have no interest in electronic computers. I do not care
about how networks work. I only think about cybersecurity when it comes to my
own usage. ***Why the fuck did I pick computer science?!***

**Because I wanted to do problem solving.**

**Because I'm *good* at problem solving.**

**Because I'm actually a mathematician, who was mislead as to what mathematics
is.**
